Time Zone

Why Time Zone were established

Until the 19th century each city had a clock with its own hour. Consequently, it was difficult to organize the means of transport among cities and countries.

In October 1884, the International Meridian Conference was held in Washington, D.C., in the United States, to establish the Time Zones System and determine a prime meridian for international use.

On the international Meridian Conference, the Greenwish Meridian was chosen as an international standard for zero degrees longitude.

To establish the time in any one place, using the Greenwich Meridian as a reference, a clock is set forward one hour for each time zone to the east, which means time gets later, and set back one hour for each time zone to the west, which means time gets earlier.
